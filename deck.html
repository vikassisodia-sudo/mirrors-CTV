07 · Challenges
Why Content Reporting is Challenging

The structural complexity of CTV makes transparent reporting uniquely difficult

📡
Fragmented Data Sources
Data flows through multiple layers — CTV device, SSP, DSP, enrichment layer — each with its own schema and latency. Reconciliation is manual and error-prone.
🔒
Publisher Data Privacy
Publishers restrict granular content-level data for competitive reasons. CTV content signals are often aggregated or anonymized before reaching the buy-side.
🧩
No Unified ID Standard
CTV lacks a cross-platform identity standard like cookies. Matching impressions to content context across devices remains inconsistent without a universal content ID.
⏱️
Signal Latency
Content enrichment signals must process in milliseconds for pre-bid. Real-time content attribution is difficult to log accurately at this speed.
📊
Margin Attribution
When deals span multiple partners (SSP deal + DSP segment), attributing media vs. data margin across the chain creates reporting complexity for both SP and client.
🗺️
Our Approach
Mirrors uses a unified enrichment layer that tags requests with consistent content metadata — enabling cleaner segment-level reporting despite the underlying fragmentation.
